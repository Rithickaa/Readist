Description

The Girl Made of Clay by Nicole Meier is a heartfelt contemporary novel that follows Sara, a struggling artist and single mother, whose life is disrupted when her estranged father, a renowned sculptor, is badly injured and placed in her care. As she navigates the emotional weight of their complicated past, Sara is forced to confront old wounds, rediscover her creative spark, and find the strength to forgive. The story beautifully explores themes of family, healing, second chances, and the transformative power of art and love.

Meet the author

Nicole Meier is an American author known for her emotionally rich contemporary fiction that often centers around family, personal growth, and second chances. With a background in journalism and a passion for storytelling, she crafts heartfelt narratives featuring strong, relatable female leads. Her novels, including The Girl Made of Clay, The House of Bradbury, and The Second Chance Supper Club, have been praised for their warmth, depth, and insight into human relationships. Nicole lives in Oregon and continues to write stories that inspire hope, healing, and connection.

Harry Potter and Sorcerer's Stone

JK Rowling


Description

“Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone” introduces readers to Harry Potter, an orphan who discovers he is a wizard on his eleventh birthday. Whisked away to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, he makes new friends and uncovers dark secrets about his past — including the truth about a dark wizard named Voldemort. Full of magic, adventure, and wonder, this first book sets the stage for one of the most iconic fantasy series of all time.

Meet the author

J.K. Rowling is a British author who skyrocketed to fame with the Harry Potter series, which has sold over 500 million copies worldwide. Born in 1965 in England, Rowling wrote the first book while struggling as a single mother. Her success story is one of perseverance and creativity. Beyond Harry Potter, she has written adult fiction under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith. She is known for her richly detailed worlds, moral themes, and influence on a generation of readers.

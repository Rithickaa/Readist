All The Light We Cannot See

Anthony Doer


Description

“All The Light We Cannot See” is a historical novel set during World War II, following the parallel lives of Marie-Laure, a blind French girl, and Werner, a German orphan with a talent for engineering. As the war progresses, their paths converge in the occupied French town of Saint-Malo. Through lyrical prose and intricate detail, the novel explores themes of resilience, the impact of war on innocence, and the ways in which human connection endures even in darkness.

Meet the author

Anthony Doerr is an acclaimed American author known for his richly layered storytelling and evocative prose. With a background in history and science, his work often blends lyrical beauty with deep emotional insight. Doerr has received numerous awards, including the Pulitzer Prize for All the Light We Cannot See. His writing is celebrated for its attention to detail, complex characters, and its ability to illuminate the human spirit amid adversity.

Eleanor Oliphant is Completely Fine

Gail Honeyman


Description

“Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine” follows Eleanor, a socially awkward and isolated woman living a routine life in Glasgow. Beneath her peculiar habits and blunt honesty lies a deeply traumatic past she refuses to confront. When she forms an unexpected friendship with Raymond, the kind IT guy from her office, Eleanor slowly begins to open up and heal. This touching and humorous novel explores loneliness, mental health, and the transformative power of kindness.

Meet the author

Gail Honeyman is a Scottish author who gained widespread recognition with her debut novel Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine. The book won the Costa First Novel Award and became an international bestseller. Honeyman’s writing is known for its warmth, wit, and insight into human behavior. She draws on themes of trauma, resilience, and emotional connection, creating characters who are both flawed and deeply relatable.
